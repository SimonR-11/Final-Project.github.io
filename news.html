News and Developments

This is the news page. Here you can see all of the CTA's new developments.

Red Line Extension

Construction officially kicked off on the long-awaited $5.7 billion Red Line Extension. This major infrastructure project will extend the Red Line 5.5 miles south from 95th Street to 130th Street, adding four new accessible stations by 2030.

See Article
State/Lake Station Redevelopment

The historic downtown State/Lake station is undergoing a massive overhaul. The 100-year-old station was cleared for demolition to make way for a fully accessible, modern replacement featuring a sweeping glass canopy and improved platforms, slated to open in 2029.

See Article
North Side Red & Purple Modernization (RPM)

Following the massive $2.1 billion Phase One overhaul, four fully rebuilt and ADA-accessible stations (Lawrence, Argyle, Berwyn, and Bryn Mawr) on the Red Line are fully operational. Transit-oriented development projects and new community spaces under the elevated tracks are now in progress.

See Article
2026 "Refresh & Renew" Program

The CTA launched its $6.5 million "Refresh & Renew" construction season for 2026. This annual campaign focuses on deep-cleaning, repairing, painting, and improving platform amenities at 28 rail stations and 14 bus turnarounds across the city.
